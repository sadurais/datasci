Convert 'am' to a factor var, and add it to dataset for ease with fitting & plotting

mtcars$am <- as.factor(mtcars$am); levels(mtcars$am) <- c("Auto", "Manual")

Question 1: Would Auto or Manual transmission give better MPG?

We will do a Student t-test with 95% confident interval to see which one category is more likely.

t.test(mtcars$mpg ~ mtcars$am, confid.level=0.975)

## 
##  Welch Two Sample t-test
## 
## data:  mtcars$mpg by mtcars$am
## t = -3.7671, df = 18.332, p-value = 0.001374
## alternative hypothesis: true difference in means is not equal to 0
## 95 percent confidence interval:
##  -11.280194  -3.209684
## sample estimates:
##   mean in group Auto mean in group Manual 
##             17.14737             24.39231

The p-value 0.001374 and estimated means of Auto vs Manual (24.39) suggest that Manual transmission is more likely to give better MPG than Auto.

Question 2: How to quantify the MPG diff between Auto and Manual transmissions?

To answer this, we will use 'Linear Regression' and fit multiple models: first with both values of 'am', then with 'qsec' kept at a constant and seeing for 'am' vaues == 0 (Auto) and 'am' valus = 1 (Manual). We will then compare these models using 'anova'.

fit1 <- lm(mpg ~ am, data=mtcars)
fit2 <- lm(mpg ~ I(factor(am)):qsec, data=mtcars)
